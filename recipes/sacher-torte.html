Sacher Torte

Sacher torte is an Austrian chocolate cake layered with apricot preserves, making it deliciously moist.

A sacher cake is a two layer chocolate cake with apricot preserves in betweeen them. The cake batter and the preserves make it moist and delicious. It is covered in a delicious melted chocolate making it a chocoholic friendly cake.

The cake when cut is beutifully chocolate inside whit a nice shine line in the middle being the preserves. It is best served with a bit of whipped creme.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 1/2 sticks unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 5 ounces bittersweet chocolate, melted and cooled slightly
  • 1 1/4 cups apricot preserves
  • 2/3 cup light corn syrup
  • 2 tablespoons rum
  • 10 ounces bittersweet chocolate, chopped
  • Unswettened whipped cream, for serving (optional)

Make the cake:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F. Butter a 9-inch springform pan. Line the bottom of the pan with parchment paper and butter the paper. Dust the pan with flour, tapping out excess.
  2. In a large bowk using a handheld electric mixer, whip the egg whites at high speed until soft peaks form.
  3. in a small bowl, whisk the all purpose flour with the almond flour and salt. in another large bowl ž, beat the butter and sugar until fluffy. Add the yolks, one at a time, and beat ntill fluffy. Beat in the chocolate, then beat in the flours. Beat in one-fourth of the whites, then, using a spatula, fold in the rest of the whites until no streaks remain.
  4. Scrape the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top. Bake the cake in the center of the oven for 35 to 40 minutes, until a toothpick inserted in the cake comes out with a few moist crumbs attached. Let the cake cool completly. Invert the cake onto a plate and peel off the parchment. Turn the cake right side up. Using a long serated knife, cut the cake horizontally into three even layers.
  5. In a small microwave safe bowl, whisk 1/4 cup plus 2 tablespoons of the apricot preserves with 1/4 cup of water and microwave until melted.
  6. Set the bottom of the springform pan on a wire rack and set the rack on a baking sheet. Arrangge the top cake layer, cut side up, on the springform pan. Brush the cake with one-third of the melted apricot preserves. Spread 1/2 of the unmelted apricot preserves on top and cover with the middle cake layer. Brush the surface with another third of the melted preserves and spread another 1/2 cup of the unmelted preserves on top. Brush the cut side of the final layer with the remaining melted preserves and set it cut side down on the cake. Using a serrated knife, trim the cake edges if necessary to even them out.

Press the preserves through a strainer to remove the solids. brush the preserves all over the cake until completly coated. Refrigerate for 20 minutes until set.

Slowly pour half of the warm chocolate glaze in the center of the cake, allowing it to gently coat the op and spread down the side. Spread the glaze for a few seconds and repeat pouring and spreading. Scrape up any excess glaze. Refrigerate for at least 10 minutes to set the glaze, then cut the torte into wedges and serve with whipped cream.
